About

Hi! My name is Lindsey Tepfer, and I'm a PhD candidate at Dartmouth College. Alongside my colleagues at the Social Computation, Representation, and Predicton Laboratory (led by Mark Thornton), I work on characterizing the various behaviors that we engage in when we interact with others. Much of this work relies on deploying deep learning models to automatically annotate audio and video recordings of people in conversation, which we then analyze to detect various patterns, such as bodily movement, speech idiosyncrasies, and how people influence each others opinions as they speak.

I'm particularly interested in the human ability to understand the minds of others, otherwise known as “theory of "mind” or “mentalizing”. More specifically, I’d like to know how we might collaborate to accomplish this task (like when we gossip about someone, for example). To investigate these questions, I’m looking at dyadic conversations between two people that are talking about a third person; this way, I can look at how their opinions of the target individual change (or don’t!) as they communicate.

Beyond that, I'm a watercolor + ink artist, as well as a pixel artist. When I'm not working on something creative, I'm usually spending time with my husband and my two pitties, lifting weights, doing Muay Thai, playing video games, or watching anime (recommendations are always accepted).
